# Lean PRD — AI Training Atlas

## Product purpose

AI Training Atlas helps AI-literate newcomers understand how the training-data, post-training, evaluation, and agent-environment ecosystem fits together. It also demonstrates rigorous industry research, information architecture, and product implementation.

## Audience

- Primary: a reader familiar with AI who is new to the industry structure.
- Secondary: AI professionals seeking orientation and portfolio reviewers evaluating the research.

## First-milestone problem

Information about this ecosystem is fragmented across technical writing, company marketing, benchmarks, and news. Readers need a single map that separates lifecycle concepts, company roles, and sourced claims without implying more certainty than the evidence supports.

## First-milestone experience

The application provides:

- A homepage explaining the project and model-development lifecycle.
- One representative foundations module.
- Twelve searchable glossary entries.
- Profiles for Mercor, AfterQuery, and Fleet.
- A qualitative cluster map.
- A research-coverage dashboard.
- Three curated research-watch items.
- Search spanning all content types.

## Product principles

1. Explain the system before listing its participants.
2. Prefer primary sources and distinguish company statements from reporting.
3. Make uncertainty visible rather than filling gaps with estimates.
4. Use interactive elements only when they improve orientation.
5. Keep content editable in Git and architecture ready for future ingestion.

## Out of scope

No database, authentication, user accounts, CMS, automated RSS ingestion, exhaustive company coverage, hosted search, company comparison, market-size estimates, or quantitative positioning map.

## Success criteria

- Every planned route loads and navigation has no broken links.
- Company facts are supported or explicitly unavailable.
- Search works across glossary and the full content index.
- Desktop and mobile layouts remain usable and readable.
- Keyboard navigation, text alternatives, and visible focus states work.
- Lint, type-check, browser tests, accessibility checks, and production build pass.

## Future milestones

1. Expand to ten researched company profiles.
2. Add deeper educational modules and cross-linking.
3. Add landscape filtering and richer discovery.
4. Introduce evidence-backed visual dashboards.
5. Build a resilient, editorially separate RSS pipeline.
6. Complete performance, accessibility, and deployment hardening.
