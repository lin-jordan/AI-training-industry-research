# Visual Direction — AI Training Atlas

## Design thesis

The interface should feel like a contemporary research report: editorial enough to invite reading, structured enough to support comparison, and restrained enough that evidence remains the focus. References include industry-intelligence products, documentation systems, editorial charts, and benchmark pages, without reproducing any one source.

## Principles

- Use hierarchy, alignment, and rules before decoration.
- Keep high information density legible through consistent spacing.
- Distinguish metadata from narrative with typography, not low contrast.
- Treat uncertainty labels and captions as first-class interface content.
- Use motion only for short state feedback and respect reduced-motion settings.

## Typography

- Geist Sans: headings, body text, navigation, and controls.
- Geist Mono: dates, labels, statuses, metrics, and source markers.
- Large headlines use tight leading and balanced line wrapping.
- Body copy targets comfortable 60–75 character reading widths.
- Avoid text smaller than 11px except nonessential visual metadata.

## Color strategy

- Background: warm off-white rather than pure white.
- Foreground: near-black for strong reading contrast.
- Blue: navigation, citations, and educational emphasis.
- Amber: reporting labels, caveats, and research uncertainty.
- Slate-gray borders organize density without card shadows.
- Do not use neon gradients, low-contrast gray-on-gray, or ornamental color.

## Spacing and layout

- Maximum content width: 1200px.
- Major sections use 48–72px vertical spacing on desktop and 36–52px on mobile.
- Use grid-aligned panels with shared borders to create report-like tables.
- Mobile layouts collapse to one column without hiding source or status information.

## Cards and panels

- Use square or lightly rounded corners, thin borders, and flat surfaces.
- Prefer connected grids to floating rounded cards.
- Every card should have a clear information job: summary, comparison, status, or navigation.
- Avoid glass effects, large shadows, and repeated decorative containers.

## Tables

- Use semantic table markup, captions, column headers, and row headers.
- Keep source and status labels readable without relying on color alone.
- Permit horizontal overflow on narrow screens rather than crushing content.

## Charts and diagrams

- Every diagram needs a caption and text equivalent.
- Qualitative maps must state that size and distance are not quantitative.
- Do not visualize funding, valuation, or market share until evidence is comparable.
- Use restrained fills and direct labels; avoid decorative 3D or unlabeled axes.

## Interaction

- Focus states use a high-contrast blue outline.
- Hover behavior must have an equivalent focus or touch path.
- Search always reports result counts and includes a clear empty state.
- External links identify themselves visually and open safely.

## Do

- Use captions, source markers, compact pills, and section numbering.
- Make research dates and uncertainty visible.
- Let white space separate ideas while borders organize details.
- Write descriptive links and preserve normal browser behavior.

## Avoid

- Generic SaaS hero copy and oversized calls to action.
- Glassmorphism, crypto-dashboard styling, and gratuitous animation.
- Tiny labels, hidden caveats, fake precision, or fabricated charts.
- Treating company marketing language as independently confirmed fact.
